# COMMUNITY GROUP GUIDE
## Ephesians, Pt. 2: Identity Inhabited
## Ephesians 1:17-23 + 2:21-22 | Garden City Church

---

## LEADER NOTE
This guide helps your group move from *hearing* about inhabitation to actually *doing* it together. The goal isn't more information — it's helping people cross the threshold into the identity they already possess. Go slow. Let people sit in the questions. The silence is often where the real work happens.

---

## OPEN (10 min)

**Icebreaker — pick one:**

> "Describe a place where you felt completely at home — like you truly belonged there. What made it feel that way?"

*Or, if your group is further along:*

> "Is there an area of your life where you know the truth about yourself in your head but struggle to live from it? What does that gap feel like?"

---

## THE BIG IDEA (2 min — leader reads aloud)

The problem isn't that we don't *have* the identity. We do. Paul says in Ephesians 1:3 that we've already been blessed with every spiritual blessing in Christ. The problem is that we don't *see* it. We own the house but live on the porch.

So Paul prays — not that God would give them more, but that they would *see* what they already have. Eyes enlightened. Hearts illuminated. Identity inhabited, not just owned.

---

## READ TOGETHER

*Ephesians 1:17-23 (CSB) — have someone read it aloud slowly*

---

## DISCUSS (35-40 min)

**1. The House You're Not Living In**
Paul prays for "enlightened eyes of the heart" — suggesting the Ephesians have truth they're not yet seeing.

> Where in your life do you have a "house you're not living in" — a truth about your identity in Christ that you believe intellectually but aren't actually inhabiting emotionally or practically?

---

**2. The Enemies Are Real**
The sermon identified seven enemies of inhabitation. Read through this list together:
- Spiritual blindness (can't see the house)
- Hope-ignorance (fear of eviction keeps you from unpacking)
- Identity poverty (self-contempt — "why would God treasure me?")
- Power-ignorance (trying in your own strength, exhausted)
- Isolation (missing what's only seen together)
- Competing powers (rival claims on your loyalty and attention)
- Fear and accusation ("you don't belong here")

> Which of these enemies feels most active in your life right now? Which one keeps you on the porch?

---

**3. The Inheritance Goes Both Ways**
Paul says in verse 18 that God has a "glorious inheritance in the saints." That's stunning — *we* are God's inheritance. He treasures us the way we treasure what we inherit.

> What does it do to you to hear that you are God's treasure — not just that he's yours? How does that truth sit with you? Does it feel true, or does something resist it?

---

**4. The Power Is Aimed at You**
Verse 19 says the "immeasurable greatness of his power" is directed *toward us who believe.* The same power that raised Jesus from the dead is aimed at you. Not at impressive Christians. At you.

> Where do you need to stop straining on your own and actually let resurrection power do what you can't? What would it look like to trust that power this week?

---

**5. You Can't Inhabit Alone**
The inheritance is perceived "in the saints" — in community. Some things about our identity can only be seen together.

> Has anyone in this group helped you see something true about yourself that you couldn't see alone? What did that feel like? Who in this room might need you to name something true about them?

---

**6. The Story You're Living In**
The GardenCity metanarrative runs from creation to new creation — from a garden where God walked with his people to a city where God dwells with his people forever. We are in Act 5: the church.

> What does it change for you to see your ordinary life as part of that story? When you show up to work on Monday, or parent your kids, or serve in this church — how does the GardenCity arc give that meaning?

---

## APPLY (10 min)

**This week's one move:**

> Pick ONE enemy of inhabitation from the list above — the one that resonates most. Write it down. Then write one truth from this passage that directly opposes it. Read that truth every morning this week before you check your phone.

Example: *Enemy: Identity poverty / "I'm not worth much."*
*Truth: "I am his glorious inheritance. He treasures me." (Eph 1:18)*

---

## CLOSE IN PRAYER

Have someone pray through the actual text of Ephesians 1:17-19 as a prayer *for the group* — substituting "us" and "we" for "you":

> *"Father of glory, give us the Spirit of wisdom and revelation in the knowledge of you. Enlighten the eyes of our hearts so that we may know the hope of your calling, the wealth of your glorious inheritance in us, and the immeasurable greatness of your power toward us who believe."*

Then give space for anyone to pray specifically for someone else in the room — asking God to help them see what's already true.
