# PRAYER GUIDE
## Ephesians, Pt. 2: Identity Inhabited
## Ephesians 1:17-23 | GardenCity Church
## Sunday, March 1, 2026

---

## HOW TO USE THIS GUIDE
This guide follows the arc of Paul's prayer in Ephesians 1:17-23. You can use it privately before Sunday, as a pre-service prayer team guide, or to lead your congregation in guided prayer during the service. Each section quotes the text, names what we're praying against, and gives language to pray forward.

---

## INVOCATION
*Before we ask for anything — we remember who we're talking to.*

Father of glory —
You are the source and possessor of all glory.
Your glory filled the garden.
Your glory filled the tabernacle.
Your glory filled the temple.
Your glory walked among us in flesh.
Your glory now dwells in us.

We approach you not as strangers
but as those who carry your name,
your Spirit,
your inheritance.

We are in the story.
Help us to see it.

---

## SECTION 1: PRAYING FOR SIGHT
*"I pray that the eyes of your heart may be enlightened..." — v. 18*

**What we're praying against:** Spiritual blindness. The dimness that lets us own truth without seeing it. The familiarity that makes the gospel feel ordinary. The busyness that leaves no room for perception.

**Pray:**
Lord, open what only you can open.
The eyes of our hearts are dim — not because the light is gone,
but because we haven't been looking.

Give us the Spirit of wisdom and revelation.
Not more information —
*sight.*

Where we have believed without seeing,
where we have confessed without feeling,
where we have owned the house but lived on the porch —

Enlighten us.
Let us see what is already true.

---

## SECTION 2: PRAYING AGAINST FEAR AND ACCUSATION
*"The hope of his calling..." — v. 18*

**What we're praying against:** The voice that says we don't belong. Assurance struggles. The fear of eviction from grace. The sense that our sin is louder than his call.

**Pray:**
Father, your calling is irrevocable.
What you have called, you keep.
What you have chosen, you do not abandon.

Silence the accuser.
Silence the voice that says "you don't belong here."
Silence the fear that keeps your people
from unpacking in the house you built for them.

Let us know — *really know* —
the hope of your calling.
Not hoping we're called.
*Knowing.*

For your people this week who are haunted by old sin,
who feel too broken to be treasured,
who are living like tenants in a house they own:

Let hope land.

---

## SECTION 3: PRAYING AGAINST IDENTITY POVERTY
*"The wealth of his glorious inheritance in the saints..." — v. 18*

**What we're praying against:** Self-contempt. The sense of worthlessness. The exhausting performance of earning love already given. The lie that says "why would God treasure me?"

**Pray:**
God, you have a glorious inheritance.
And it is *us.*

We confess how hard that is to believe.
We confess the self-contempt we carry.
We confess the ways we shrink from being called your treasure
because it feels too good to be true.

For the person in this room who hates themselves —
who has believed the worst about who they are —
who has worked endlessly to earn what you've already given:

Let them receive.
Let them be undone by being treasured.
Let identity poverty end here.

We are not poor.
We are *your glorious inheritance.*
Make us brave enough to believe it.

---

## SECTION 4: PRAYING FOR POWER
*"The immeasurable greatness of his power toward us who believe..." — v. 19*

**What we're praying against:** Self-reliance. Exhaustion from straining in our own strength. Practical atheism — the life planned without prayer, the change attempted without trust.

**Pray:**
The same power that raised Jesus from the dead
is aimed at us.
*Toward us who believe.*

We confess that we've been trying in our own strength.
We confess that we've admired resurrection power in history
without accessing it personally.
We confess that we've made plans and forgotten to pray.

We are tired, Lord.
Some of us are very tired.

So we stop straining.
We open our hands.
We ask for the immeasurable.

For every area where we have given up
because we lacked the power to change:

Come with your power.
Come, Holy Spirit.
We are not strong enough.
We don't need to be.

---

## SECTION 5: PRAYING FOR THE CHURCH TO BE THE CHURCH
*"The church, which is his body, the fullness of the one who fills all in all." — v. 23*

**What we're praying against:** Isolation. Consumerism. The fractured individualism that treats church as a service rather than a family. The wounds that keep people from community.

**Pray:**
Lord, you made us a body.
Not a crowd.
Not an audience.
A *body* — connected, interdependent, each part necessary.

Forgive us for treating community as optional.
Forgive us for the wounds we've inflicted on each other
that have made this gathering feel unsafe.

Heal what is broken in this room between people.
Restore what church hurt has stolen.
Draw back the isolated ones.
Make us the kind of community
where inhabitation is possible
because the house feels safe.

Make GardenCity Church
what you called it to be —
your dwelling place in this city.
Christ's fullness, expressed here.
The GardenCity, begun now.

---

## CLOSING BLESSING
*Pray over the congregation as they go:*

May the Father of glory
open the eyes of your hearts
to the hope you were called to,
the inheritance you already are,
and the power that is already aimed at you.

May you inhabit what you possess.
May you live in the house you own.
May you know — really know —
that you are not a stranger here.

You belong.
You are his.
Go live like it.

Amen.
