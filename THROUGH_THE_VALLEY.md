# THROUGH THE VALLEY
## A Grief Companion System
## Vision by Curtis Gilbert | Built by J5 | 2026-02-28

---

## WHERE THIS CAME FROM
Curtis lost both parents. In the moment after death, he needed someone to take his hand — not just to cry with, but to walk him through the flood of practical questions nobody prepares you for. He didn't have that. This is built so nobody else has to navigate that moment alone.

This is not primarily a revenue play. It is a calling that happens to have a product shape.

---

## THE PROBLEM
The moment someone you love dies, you need simultaneously:
- A grief counselor (to feel what you're feeling)
- A pastor (to hold eternal perspective)
- A lawyer (to explain wills, trusts, probate in plain English)
- A financial advisor (accounts, benefits, insurance claims)
- A funeral director's assistant (logistics, decisions, coordination)
- A creative director (photos, slideshow, obituary, program)
- A project manager (the 40 things that have to happen in the next 72 hours)
- A long-term companion (month 3, month 6, the first Christmas without them)

Nobody has all of these. Most people have none.
The result: families make expensive mistakes, miss important deadlines, feel completely overwhelmed, and carry practical chaos on top of raw grief.

---

## THE VISION: THROUGH THE VALLEY

A comprehensive grief companion that walks alongside someone from the moment of death through the first year — and beyond if needed.

Warm. Practical. Pastoral. Personal.

Not a cold checklist. Not a law firm website. Not a grief hotline.
A hand. Extended. At the exact moment you need it.

## THE FOUR PHASES

### Phase 1 — The First 72 Hours (Immediate)
*The fog is thickest here. Decisions get made badly or not at all.*

What the system does:
- Gently surfaces the immediate priority list in bite-sized pieces
- Explains what NOT to do (don't cancel accounts, don't make financial decisions, don't give things away yet)
- Tells you exactly how many death certificates to order and why
- Walks through who to call and in what order
- Helps draft the first announcement to family and friends (in your voice)
- Coordinates meal trains and help offers so you don't have to manage that too
- Creates a simple funeral planning guide customized to your situation

What it feels like:
Not a dashboard. A conversation. "I know this is overwhelming. Let's just do the next one thing."

---

### Phase 2 — The First Month (Practical Navigation)
*The casseroles stop. The real work starts.*

What the system does:
- Probate explained in plain English — do you need it, what does it cost, how long does it take
- Will and trust walkthrough — what you have, what it means, what to bring to an attorney
- Financial accounts — joint accounts, individual accounts, what happens to each
- Life insurance claims — step by step
- Benefits navigation — Social Security survivor benefits, pension, employer death benefits
- Digital estate — social media, email, subscriptions, streaming services
- Employer and HR notifications
- Thank you notes (drafts generated in your voice for review)
- Obituary writing assistance

What it feels like:
A knowledgeable friend who happens to know all the things — explaining without jargon, guiding without rushing.

---

### Phase 3 — Months 2-6 (The Long Middle)
*When everyone else has moved on and you're still grieving.*

What the system does:
- Grief check-ins — not clinical, just present. "How are you doing this week?"
- Flags approaching milestones: their birthday, your anniversary, Father's Day, Mother's Day
- Helps you process secondary losses (their voicemail you can't bring yourself to delete, selling their house, clearing their belongings)
- Continues to walk through any unfinished practical tasks
- Connects to grief counseling resources when professional support is warranted
- Gently helps you think about legacy: what do you want to preserve of them?

What it feels like:
Someone who remembers. Who doesn't forget that you're still carrying this.

---

### Phase 4 — The First Year and Beyond (Legacy + Healing)
*The firsts: first Christmas, first birthday, first anniversary of their death.*

What the system does:
- Proactively reaches out before hard dates
- Helps create lasting legacy artifacts: a memory book, a photo archive, a recorded family story project
- Annual anniversary check-in — "It's been a year. How are you?"
- Connects family members to each other across the grief experience
- Helps you think about honoring them going forward

What it feels like:
They haven't been forgotten. Neither have you.

## THE VOICE AND TONE
This system never feels clinical. It never feels like a checklist. It never rushes.

Every interaction is:
- **Warm** — you are not a case number
- **Present** — meeting you exactly where you are
- **Patient** — grief has no timeline
- **Clear** — practical information without overwhelming
- **Pastoral** — holding eternal perspective without being preachy
- **Honest** — not minimizing the loss or rushing to silver linings

Curtis's voice is in the DNA of this. He lost his parents. He knows what this moment feels like. That shapes everything.

AI does the infrastructure. Humans do the irreplaceable parts.

This system connects to:
- **Grief counselors** (referral network, warm handoff when professional care is needed)
- **Estate attorneys** (directory of vetted, plain-English communicators)

## THE WORKSHOP SERIES
*Grief education people want BEFORE they need it.*

Most people won't download a grief app until someone dies. But they'll show up to a Tuesday night workshop at their church when they're 45, their parents are aging, and they realize they have no idea what probate means.

### The Five Workshops:

**1. Before the Loss — Get Ready Now**
Wills, trusts, end-of-life conversations, powers of attorney, what to have in place so your family isn't scrambling. The conversation most families avoid until it's too late.

**2. The First 72 Hours — What To Do When Someone Dies**
Exactly what to do, in order, when it happens. Who to call. What not to do. What decisions can wait. The practical hand nobody extends.

**3. Navigating the Estate — Plain English**
Probate, accounts, benefits, life insurance claims, digital estate. All of it explained without jargon by people who've been through it.

**4. Grief That Lasts — The Long Middle**
The casseroles stop. Everyone moves on. You don't. Secondary losses, the first year, the firsts — birthday, Christmas, anniversary of the death. What healthy grief actually looks like.

**5. Legacy While You Can**
Recording family stories before it's too late. Legacy letters. What you want to leave. The conversations most families never have and always wish they did.

### Each Workshop Includes:
- Live event at GardenCity Church (test bed for everything)
- Recorded and packaged as a digital course
- Workbook — actual forms, checklists, templates, scripts
- Three voices in the room: Curtis (pastoral + personal loss experience), a vetted estate attorney, a licensed grief counselor

### Why Three Voices?
Because nobody has all three. The attorney doesn't know how to hold grief. The counselor doesn't know probate. The pastor doesn't know estate law. Together they cover every dimension of what a family actually needs. Curtis is the connective tissue — the person who lost his parents, figured it out the hard way, and built the thing he needed.

### The Rollout:
