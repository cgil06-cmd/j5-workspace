# Ephesians Series Debrief & Spiritual Formation Reflection
**Date:** None
**Attendees:** Curtis Gilbert, Lovett, Penta, The Rollers, Unnamed team members

## Executive Summary
Curtis and his team reflected on the completed Ephesians sermon series at Garden City Church, focusing on the shift from 'knowing identity' to 'inhabiting identity.' The group discussed how God's presence was evident through unexpected alignments between Sunday messages on friction and the Marriage Builders event, personal spiritual breakthroughs centered on repentance and Ephesians 2:13, and the church's unique identity formation through diverse practices. Curtis's vulnerable leadership throughout the series was noted as particularly impactful in helping the congregation feel they belonged while being formed collectively by God.

## Key Decisions
- Affirmed the theological direction of transitioning from knowing to inhabiting identity as foundational for Garden City's future
- Recognized the need to continue emphasizing practices that promote belonging and diverse inclusion in the church community

## Themes
- Spiritual formation through vulnerability and authenticity in leadership
- Divine alignment and orchestration across multiple church settings and messaging
- Repentance and cleansing as prerequisites for genuine worship
- Individual identity and collective church identity as interconnected
- Practical application of theological teaching through experiential practices
- Belonging and inclusion as central to discipleship
